The Human Digestive System

Mouth

Where digestion begins! Teeth chew food, and saliva starts breaking down carbohydrates.

Esophagus

A muscular tube that pushes food from the mouth to the stomach using wave-like contractions.

Stomach

Produces acid and enzymes to break down proteins. It churns food into a liquid called chyme.

Small Intestine

Where most nutrient absorption happens. Enzymes from the pancreas and bile from the liver help digest food.

Large Intestine

Absorbs water and electrolytes from undigested food, forming waste products.

Liver

Produces bile to help digest fats and detoxifies harmful substances in the blood.

Pancreas

Produces enzymes that break down carbohydrates, proteins, and fats in the small intestine.

Gallbladder

Stores and concentrates bile produced by the liver, releasing it into the small intestine.

Rectum

The final part of the large intestine that stores feces before elimination.

Anus

The opening at the end of the digestive tract where waste is expelled from the body.
